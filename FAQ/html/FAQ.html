Frequently Asked Questions





How do I get to Ireland?

Ireland is accessible by air and sea. Major airlines operate international flights to Dublin, Cork, Belfast, Shannon, Knock, Derry and Kerry airports. Ferries are available from Britan, France and Spain to Dublin, Roslare, Cork and Belfast seaports.

What currency is used in Ireland?

The currency used in Ireland the Euro. In the north of Ireland the official curreny is Sterling but the Euro is also accepted for card payments.

What are the duty-free options?

Duty-free shopping is available at airports and ports for passengers arriving from non EU countries. You can purchase a variety of goods including alcohol, tobacco, and perfumes at reduced prices.

What are the car rental options?

All major car rental companies operate in Ireland including EuroCar, Hertz, AVIS, Enterprise, Sixt, Alamo and Budget.

What are the public transport options?

There are a number of bus companies operating at a national level between all major towns and cities as well as local bus companies providing rural services. In addition to this a number of specialised bus tour operators offer single and multiple day tour options. Irish Rail provide train links to most major towns and cities.
